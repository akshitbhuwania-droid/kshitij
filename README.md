<h2 style="color:#781A1A;margin-top:100px;margin-left:25px"> About India's Famous Place</h2>
<p style="font-size:20;">India’s landscape is a masterclass in historical layering. The Taj Mahal (Agra) remains the gold standard for symmetry and Mughal elegance, while Hampi’s boulder-strewn ruins (Karnataka) preserve the skeletal remains of a vanished 14th-century empire. Spiritual centers like the Golden Temple (Amritsar) and Varanasi’s riverfront ghats offer visceral, living culture, contrasting with the ancient, silent rock-cut mastery of the Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra). From the high-altitude peaks of Sikkim to the rhino-guarded plains of Kaziranga (Assam), India's famous sites blend man-made grandeur with raw, natural heritage. Recent UNESCO additions like the Moidams of Assam and the Maratha Military Landscapes further highlight the country's diverse architectural evolution. This unique fusion of living traditions and ancient monuments ensures that every corner of the subcontinent tells a distinct story of human achievement.</p>

<h1 style="color:#900D09;text-align:center;text-decoration: underline silver;margin-top:50px"> Taj Mahal </h1>
<img src="C:/Users/AMIT-DELL/OneDrive/Desktop/Computer Project/INDIA.jpg" style="margin-top:8px;margin-left:450px;border-color:#781A1A;border-style:solid;border-width:5px;" width=360 height=235 > </img>
<p style="margin-top:40px;font-size:20;margin-left:">The Taj Mahal is a breathtaking ivory-white marble mausoleum located on the south bank of the Yamuna River in Agra, India. Commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a tribute to his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal, the monument is universally celebrated as a symbol of eternal love and devotion. Its architectural brilliance lies in the seamless fusion of Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles, characterized by flawless symmetry, a massive central dome, and four surrounding minarets.The construction of this UNESCO World Heritage Site required labor of over 20,000 artisans and approximately 22 years to complete, incorporating intricate Pietra Dura, (marble inlay) with 28 types of precious and semi-precious stones.Renowned as one of the New 7 Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal's marble surfaces famously shift in color from pinkish in the morning to golden under the moonlight, leaving millions of annual visitors mesmerized by its ever-changing beauty.
